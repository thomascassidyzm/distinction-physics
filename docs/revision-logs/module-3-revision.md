## Critical Principle: RELOCATES, NOT SOLVES

The most important revision principle for Module 3:

> **The framework RELOCATES the hard problem of consciousness. It does NOT solve, dissolve, or explain it away.**

Previous language sometimes implied the framework "transforms" or "dissolves" the hard problem in a way that could be read as claiming a solution. The revision makes explicit:

- We relocate the question from "how does matter generate experience?" to "why does self-referential boundary maintenance have an inside perspective?"
- This relocation may or may not be philosophically satisfying
- We do NOT explain why consciousness exists
- We do NOT derive qualia from physics
- We offer philosophical vocabulary, not proof

## Summary of Epistemic Categories in Module 3

### What We INTERPRET (philosophical vocabulary):
- Consciousness as self-referential distinction-making
- The self/not-self boundary as primordial distinction
- Qualia as interior perspective on boundary maintenance
- Attention as resource allocation for distinction-maintenance
- Free will as self-referential evaluation (compatibilism)
- Conscious states as varying energy investments
- Unity as thermodynamic optimization

### What We IMPORT (from neuroscience and other sciences):
- ~20W brain power consumption
- ~8W estimate for conscious processing
- Neural correlates of consciousness
- Global workspace theory (Baars)
- Binding phenomena
- Working memory limits, attentional blink, inattentional blindness
- Split-brain and anesthesia effects
- Landauer's principle (from thermodynamics)

### What We CONJECTURE (speculative):
- The ~10^7 complexity threshold
- Assessments of animal consciousness
- Assessments of AI/machine consciousness
- The levels of self-referential complexity (0-6)

### What Remains GENUINELY OPEN:
- Why consciousness exists at all (the hard problem is relocated, not solved)
- Why specific qualia have their specific character
- The precise mechanisms of binding
- The exact threshold for consciousness
- Which non-human systems are conscious
