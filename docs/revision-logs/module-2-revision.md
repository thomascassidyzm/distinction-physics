## Overview

This revision applies the meta-insights from the foundational documents to Module 2 (Mathematics). The key insight driving this revision is that mathematics **falls out from** distinction thinking rather than being **independently derived** from the two axioms.

The fundamental change is from overclaiming ("we derive mathematics from distinction") to accurate positioning ("mathematics is consistent with distinction-primacy").

---

## Core Principle Applied

**Before:** Module 2 claimed to derive or show the "emergence" of mathematics from distinction-making axioms, implying that number, set, logic, geometry, and probability follow necessarily from the two axioms.

**After:** Module 2 demonstrates that mathematical structures are **consistent with** distinction-primacy and **fall out naturally** when thinking in terms of distinctions. This is interpretation, not derivation.

The key distinction:
- We do NOT claim to derive mathematics from scratch
- We show that mathematical structure is CONSISTENT with the framework
- Mathematics exists independently; we offer a lens for understanding it
- The relationship is complementary, not replacement
